FIRST PRINCIPLES
How does a brand actually make money?
There are only three steps. You have to get all three right — and only one matters in the end.
Step 1
A person sees you
Step 2
They visit the store
Step 3 · The one that counts
They buy the product
All three steps matter. But until money comes in, nothing else does.
You can get a million people to see your ad. You can get a million to visit the site. But if zero buy, you have zero. The whole point — every angle, every test, every choice — is to get someone to pull out their card.

How to scale: copy what already works.
The easiest path is to copy competitors who are scaling. If they're spending big and growing, the format works. Take their structure, add your taste and branding, ship faster. Don't start from a blank page — start from what's already proven.
WHO ARE YOU SELLING TO?
Specific beats broad. Always.
"Women 25-65" is not a target. It's a population. You need a person — someone with a specific situation, a specific pain, a specific reason to buy today.
How to find that person — 4 places to look
01
Check your reviews
Read every 5-star review. The phrases customers use to describe their problem = your ad copy. Free, real, written by the buyer.
02
Check Reddit
Search the category on Reddit. Read threads where real people complain or ask for recs. Their language is gold — copy it directly.
03
Check competitor sites
Their product pages, reviews, ad library. See who they target, what angle works. Steal what works, skip what doesn't.
04
Look below ↓

AD → LP → PURCHASE
Three steps. Until revenue comes, nothing matters.
A perfect ad with a broken landing page = no sale. A perfect landing page with a weak ad = no traffic. Each step has to hand off cleanly to the next. The whole chain only counts when money lands.
Step 1
Ad gets the click
Hook, copy, image. Stop the scroll and earn the next tap.
Step 2
LP earns the add-to-cart
Landing page delivers what the ad promised. Same tone, same angle, same hero.
Step 3
Checkout closes
Frictionless flow, trust signals, easy payment. Money lands.
Focus on Step 1. But ad → LP coherence is what closes the sale.
You spend your time on the ad — that's the lever you control. Scalers handle the LP and checkout. But the handoff matters: if your ad promises "lemon-shaped pleasure" and the landing page opens with a medical-grade silicone spec sheet, the buyer bounces. Same voice. Same hero. Same promise. That's coherence.

Let's dive into the Ads Account
This is where the money actually moves.
Every brand has a Meta Business Manager and one or more ad accounts inside. The ad account is where you launch campaigns, set budgets, and watch results in real time. Today we open it up and walk through what each piece does.
Three things to know first
  • Structure — Campaign → Ad Set → Ad. Campaign holds the objective, ad set holds the audience and budget, ads are the creatives.
  • Spend — Daily budget is the dial. Start small, scale what works. Never over-commit on day 1.
  • Signal — Meta needs 50 purchases in a week per ad set to optimize properly. Below that, the algorithm is guessing.
Walk-through · live · in Ads Manager · open your account before this slide
Campaign Setup Rules
Naming. Structure. Country.
Three rules every campaign follows. Get them wrong and the data is unreadable, the spend is wrong, or the audience is wrong. Get them right and the team can scale together.
Rule 1 · Naming
Follow existing if it exists.
If a campaign is already running for your brand, match its naming convention exactly. Don't invent a new one. Unsure? Ask your scaler or Momo before you ship.
New campaign? Use this format:
AdAccount_LandingPage_Country_Date
Example: HelloNancy_Zenify_Top15_2026-05-14
Rule 2 · Structure
CBO + one ad set per concept.
Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO) on. Set the budget at the campaign level — Meta allocates spend across ad sets automatically based on performance.
One ad set per concept. Each ad set holds one angle / one creative theme. Inside the ad set, run 2-3 variations of that concept.
Why CBO: simpler to manage, Meta finds the winning ad set faster, you don't waste mental cycles re-balancing budgets manually.

5 reasons to reject an ad
Fail any one → don't ship.
❌
Wrong product
Ad shows a SKU that isn't what you're selling. Confused buyer = no purchase.
🤐
Explicit copy
Words like "clit", "sex" — Meta flags it. Account gets banned, all spend stops.
🚫
Minors / under-25 look
Kids or anyone who looks under 25 in a sex-toy brand ad. Auto-reject. Account-killer.
⚠️
False claims
"Cures menopause", "doctors recommend", "best in the world" — anything unprovable.
📱
Bad on placements
Cut off on Reels or Feed. Text unreadable on Stories. Product hidden on Marketplace.
When in doubt — reject and rebuild.
A bad ad live costs more than a good ad late. One banned account can kill an entire brand's spend for a week.
📐
Two formats only: 9:16 + 1:1
Every ad ships in 9:16 (vertical) for Reels/Stories and 1:1 (square) for Feed. No other ratios. If you're sizing down from 9:16 to 1:1, make sure all key content is visible in the square crop — otherwise make two separate versions.
Before You Make a Single Ad
Pre-Flight Checklist
Don't touch Figma or Gemini until you've done these five things. ~1 hour total. This is where good ads come from.
1
Study your brand
What's the voice? What's the promise? What makes this not just another product? Read the site. Read the reviews. Know it cold.
~15 min
2
Study your competitors
Open Meta Ad Library. What are competitors running? What angles, hooks, formats? Where are the gaps you can exploit?
~15 min
3
Identify your hero product
Pick the one SKU with the best margin + best story + best proof. That's your lead. Everything else comes after.
~10 min
4
Mine customer language
Reddit, Amazon reviews, Trustpilot, customer tickets. Find the exact words real customers use to describe the problem and the relief. Steal their language — don't invent copywriter language.
~20 min
5
Find 3 winning competitor ads
Screenshot them from Meta Ad Library. These are your visual reference — way easier to remix than to start from a blank canvas. If they're spending big, the format works.
~10 min · then you're ready
